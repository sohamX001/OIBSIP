APJ Abdul Kalam
15 Oct, 1931 – 27 July, 2015

“Dream, Dream, Dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.”

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, an extraordinary scientist, teacher, and the 11th President of India. Known as the "Missile Man of India," Dr. Kalam left an indelible mark on the world through his contributions to science, his vision for a better future, and his unwavering commitment to inspiring the younger generation.

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Abdul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam emerged from humble beginnings to become one of India's most beloved and respected figures. His journey exemplifies the power of perseverance, self-belief, and dedication to making a positive impact on society. Dr. Kalam's passion for science led him to pursue a career in aeronautical engineering. He played a pivotal role in India's space and missile programs, contributing significantly to the development of indigenous satellite launch vehicles and ballistic missiles. Driven by his conviction that science and technology can drive social and economic progress, he envisioned a self-reliant India, equipped with advanced scientific capabilities.

Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam possessed an extraordinary ability to connect with people from all walks of life, especially the youth. He dedicated a significant portion of his life to nurturing young minds, encouraging them to dream big and pursue their aspirations fearlessly. Dr. Kalam firmly believed that education, innovation, and perseverance were the keys to unlocking India's potential and transforming it into a developed nation. In 2002, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam assumed the role of the President of India, serving as the nation's first citizen. During his tenure, he endeared himself to the nation with his humility, accessibility, and inspirational speeches. He traveled extensively, engaging with students, scientists, and citizens, emphasizing the importance of unity, compassion, and national development.

Despite his numerous accolades, Dr. Kalam remained a humble and down-to-earth individual throughout his life. His simplicity, integrity, and dedication to public service continue to inspire millions around the globe. Even after his presidency, he remained committed to igniting the minds of young people, tirelessly working to foster scientific temper and social progress. On July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam left this mortal world, leaving behind a profound void. However, his vision and teachings continue to guide us, urging us to strive for excellence, contribute to society, and embrace knowledge as a transformative force.
